Understanding Atrial Fibrillation

Atrial Fibrillation (AF or AFib) is a common cardiac arrhythmia (irregular heart rhythm) characterized by rapid and disorganized electrical signals in the atria, the upper chambers of the heart. This leads to an irregular and often rapid heartbeat, which may cause symptoms such as:

  • Palpitations
  • Fatigue
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizziness
